Tasty Fusion

UI/UX Project 1

Project Scope

For this UI/UX design project, Tasty Fusion, I created a user-friendly recipe website design targeting three main target audiences, home cooks, busy professionals, and health-conscious individuals. I began with UX research, and developed personas to understand user needs. I created moodboards and logos to establish the visual style and brand identity. Following this, I designed lo-fi prototypes to map out the basic structure, then developed hi-fi prototypes with detailed design elements. Finally, I made interactive versions to test usability. This project covered all design stages, from initial research and moodboards to final interactive prototypes, aiming to make finding and cooking recipes enjoyable and accessible for everyone.

Goal

The goal of this project was to design a visually appealing and user-friendly recipe website design for target markets. Using UX principles, I aimed to create a seamless user experience and create interactive prototypes using Figma.

Planning

During the planning phase for this project, I conducted UX research and then crafted detailed personas to understand user needs. I developed moodboards to define the visual direction and designed logos to create a strong brand identity. Lo-fi prototypes were created to outline the website's structure, laying the groundwork for the subsequent hi-fi and interactive prototypes. This comprehensive planning ensured a cohesive and user-focused design.

Process

I started the project by identifying target markets and creating detailed personas to understand user needs. Next, I developed moodboards and color palettes to establish the visual style and designed the logo for a strong brand identity. Following this, I created lo-fi prototypes to map out the website structure and hi-fi prototypes to refine the design. Finally, I built interactive prototypes to test usability and ensure a seamless user experience.

Target Market and Personas

Target Market

Primary Target Market: Home Cooks and Food Enthusiasts
Individuals who love cooking as a hobby and enjoy trying new recipes. They seek detailed instructions, high-quality images, and videos to enhance their culinary skills.

Secondary Target Market: Busy Professionals
Professionals with hectic schedules looking for quick, easy-to-make recipes. They value meal prep tips and efficient cooking methods that save time without compromising health and taste.

Tertiary Target Market: Health-Conscious Individuals
Individuals focused on maintaining a healthy diet. They seek recipes that cater to specific dietary needs like gluten-free, vegan, keto, or paleo, and appreciate nutritional information and wholesome ingredients.

Personas

I developed detailed personas to better understand and cater to the diverse user base. These personas include individuals passionate about cooking, busy professionals prioritizing healthy eating, and health-conscious individuals committed to specific dietary lifestyles.

PetCare

App Design Project

Project Scope and Goal

This was a group project named PetCare. It is a comprehensive mobile application designed to cater to the diverse needs of pet owners, focusing on pet health, grooming, finding veterinarians, and other essential services. This group project aims to create an intuitive and user-friendly platform that simplifies the management of pet care activities. The primary goal is to enhance the user experience by providing seamless access to a wide range of pet-related services, ensuring that pet owners can effortlessly take care of their beloved pets' well-being.

Planning & Process

During the planning phase of our project, we team conducted UX research, including and interviews with pet owners, to identify their specific needs and challenges. This research helped us ensuring our design was user-centric and addressed real pain points effectively. We also established clear project timelines to keep the project on track.

Then, we developed lo-fi prototypes and then high-fidelity prototypes incorporating detailed design elements and interactions. These prototypes were rigorously tested with users to gather feedback, which was then used to refine and improve the design. The last step was to make fully interactive prototypes for our app and then we used Maze to get feedback for our design. This thorough planning process laid a strong foundation for the successful development of the PetCare app.
